Frequently Asked Questions

  • Should I only hire a private investigator who is licensed in the State of California?

    Yes. California law requires private investigators to hold a valid license issued by the Bureau of Security and Investigative Services. Hiring a licensed professional ensures they meet the necessary standards to conduct private investigations in the State of California. My private investigator license number is 013773 and can be verified at search.dca.ca.gov.

  • Why is it important for a private investigator to carry insurance?

    Although it’s not required by law, carrying liability and errors and omissions (E&O) insurance is very important. It demonstrates that a private investigator is serious, professional, and financially responsible. I carry both liability and E&O insurance to ensure that my clients are always working with a true professional they can trust.

  • What professional background and areas of specialization should I look for?

    Look for private investigators with relevant law enforcement, legal, or investigative experience related to your case type. As a retired police officer with 28 years of service and a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice, I possess the skills, knowledge, and experience to investigate a wide variety of case types.

  • Can my investigation be handled outside of regular business hours?

    Absolutely. Investigative work often requires flexibility beyond a 9–5 schedule. I offer evening, weekend, and on-call availability to meet urgent or unexpected needs.

  • How will my confidentiality be protected?

    All case files, communications, and findings are managed under strict confidentiality. Information will only be shared with you, your authorized designee, or as otherwise required by law.

  • Who will be responsible for managing and conducting my investigation?

    I will personally oversee and conduct every aspect of your case. You will have direct access to me throughout the investigation.

  • Will you coordinate with my attorney throughout the process?

    Yes. I will collaborate with your legal counsel and tailor evidence collection and reporting to support litigation or administrative proceedings as needed.

  • How frequently will I receive status updates?

    Update frequency is customized to your needs—daily briefs, weekly summaries, or immediate alerts for critical developments are all available, depending on the case type.

  • When the investigation concludes, will I receive a report?

    Yes. Upon conclusion, you will receive the type of report of your choosing—ranging from a comprehensive written report, including timelines, findings, and evidence collected, to a simple verbal summary. The choice is yours.

  • How can I be sure your methods comply with legal and ethical standards?

    All investigative activities strictly adhere to California law, BSIS regulations, and professional ethical standards. I will not employ unauthorized or illegal investigative practices.

  • If my case goes to court, can you serve as a witness?

    Yes. I am prepared and experienced in providing courtroom testimony for criminal, civil, and administrative hearings.

  • Will there be a written agreement outlining the scope, fees, and terms?

    Yes. Before any work begins, you will receive a clear, written contract detailing the scope of services, fee structure, confidentiality terms, and other conditions.

  • Can you guarantee the outcome I’m seeking?

    No. While I commit to conducting a thorough and lawful investigation, ethical standards prohibit promising specific results. My role is to uncover objective facts and deliver accurate findings.

Professional Experience

I recently retired from the California Highway Patrol after 28 years of service, and hold a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice. During my law enforcement career, I conducted both uniformed and undercover investigations, and served in roles that allowed me to investigate and supervise a broad range of criminal and administrative cases to include:

  • Auto Theft Investigations: Detection, recovery, and follow-up on stolen vehicle cases.
  • Environmental Crimes Investigations: Investigations involving hazardous materials releases and environmental violations.
