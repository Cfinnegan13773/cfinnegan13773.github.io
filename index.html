Frequently Asked Questions

  • Should I only hire a private investigator who is licensed in the State of California?

    Yes. California law requires private investigators to hold a valid license issued by the Bureau of Security and Investigative Services. Hiring a licensed professional ensures they meet the legal standards necessary to conduct private investigations in the State of California. My private investigator's license number is ######, and it can be verified at search.dca.ca.gov.

  • Should a private investigator carry insurance?

    Yes. Although it’s not required by law, carrying liability and errors & omissions (E&O) insurance is highly recommended. It demonstrates that a private investigator is serious, professional, and financially responsible. I carry both liability and E&O insurance to ensure my clients work with a qualified professional.

  • What professional background and areas of specialization should I look for?

    Look for private investigators with relevant law enforcement, legal, or investigative experience aligned with your specific case type. As a retired police officer with 28 years of service and a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice, I possess the skills, knowledge, and experience to investigate a wide variety of case types.

  • Can my investigation be handled outside of regular business hours?

    Absolutely. Investigative work often requires flexibility beyond a 9–5 schedule. I offer evening, weekend, and on-call availability to meet urgent or unexpected needs.

  • How will my confidentiality be protected?

    All case files, communications, and findings are managed under strict confidentiality. Information will only be shared with you, your authorized designee, or as otherwise required by law.

  • Who will be responsible for managing and conducting my investigation?

    I will personally manage and conduct every aspect of your case, and you will have direct access to me throughout the investigation.

  • Will you coordinate with my attorney throughout the process?

    Yes. I will work with your attorney to ensure my investigative strategy supports your overall legal objectives. I will maintain open communication with your attorney, provide timely updates, and tailor the investigation and reporting to meet the specific needs of your case.

  • When the investigation concludes, will I receive a report?

    Yes. Upon conclusion, you will receive a report of your choosing. It can be anywhere from a comprehensive written report, including timelines, findings, and evidence collected, to a simple verbal summary of my findings. Final reports are often dictated by the case type, but can be tailored to your request.

  • How can I be sure your methods comply with legal and ethical standards?

    All investigative activities strictly adhere to California law, BSIS regulations, and professional ethical standards. I will not employ unauthorized or illegal investigative practices.

  • If my case goes to court, can you serve as a witness?

    Yes. I am prepared and experienced in providing courtroom testimony for criminal, civil, and administrative hearings.

  • Will there be a written agreement outlining the scope, fees, and terms?

    Yes. Before any work begins, you will receive a clear, written agreement detailing the scope of services, fee structure, and other conditions.

  • Can you guarantee the outcome I’m seeking?

    No. While I can commit to conducting a thorough and lawful investigation, ethical standards prohibit promising specific results. My role is to uncover objective facts and deliver accurate findings.

Professional Experience

I retired from the California Highway Patrol in 2022 after 28 years of service, and I hold a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice. During my law enforcement career, I served in roles that allowed me to supervise and conduct a wide range of criminal and administrative investigations, including:

  • Personnel & Administrative Investigations: Internal investigations involving employee misconduct, excessive force, on-duty alcohol/drug impairment, fraud, theft, and workplace violence.
  • Traffic Collision Investigations: Supervision and investigation of complex collisions resulting in criminal charges of second-degree murder, hit-and-run, alcohol and/or drug-impairment, and vehicular assault.
  • Officer-Involved Shootings: Supervision and investigation of critical incidents involving the use of deadly force by law enforcement personnel.
  • Auto Theft: Supervision and investigation of the detection, recovery, and follow-up of stolen vehicles.
  • Environmental Crimes: Supervision and investigation of crimes and collisions involving the intentional and/or accidental release of hazardous materials.
  • Vehicle Inspections: Supervision and investigation of comprehensive pre-collision and post-collision vehicle inspections of both commercial and passenger vehicles.
  • Surveillance Operations: Day and night, mobile and stationary surveillance of facilities, vehicles, and people.
